Key Results

Research Findings

Six hypotheses tested across efficiency residuals, league differences, injury context, and positional wage dynamics.

✓ Supported

Performance Drives Wages

Position-specific performance metrics explain 63–71% of wage variance. Forwards show the strongest salary-performance link.

✓ Supported

Significant Efficiency Mismatch

A substantial proportion of players are systematically over- or underpaid relative to their performance output.

✓ Supported

League-Level Wage Premiums

Premier League and La Liga exhibit significant positive wage premiums vs. Bundesliga, Ligue 1 and Serie A benchmarks.

~ Partial

Injury Adjustment Effect

Injury context modestly shifts efficiency rankings, with high-injury players showing larger residual volatility on re-estimation.

✓ Supported

Positional Wage Hierarchy

Forwards command the largest wage premiums; goalkeepers exhibit the most compressed salary distributions across leagues.

~ Partial

Temporal Stability

Out-of-sample RMSE increases modestly across seasons, suggesting wage-setting norms shift faster than performance signals.
