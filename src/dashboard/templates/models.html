Economic Interpretation

What do the coefficients mean?

Since the dependent variable is log(wage), each coefficient represents an approximate percentage change in annual salary.

Age (concave effect)

The quadratic age specification captures concave productivity: wages rise through peak years (~28–30) then decline as physical performance deteriorates.

β(Age) ≈ +0.59 · β(Age²) ≈ −0.009 → peak at ~33 years for Defenders

Minutes Played % (Min_pct)

Signal of manager trust: a higher share of available minutes played signals that the coach considers the player first-choice, directly commanding a wage premium.

One percentage point increase in Min% → approx. 0.8–1.2% higher wage

Club Wage Rank (Club_Wage_Rank_Pct)

A player's within-club wage rank captures tournament theory effects: clubs pay disproportionately for perceived top earners, creating convex pay structures.

Strongest for Forwards — the #1 earner at a club earns 40–80% more than #5

Non-Penalty Goals per 90 (NPGls_p90)

Goals remain the most legible signal of forward value — clubs pay heavily for scoring records, even controlling for shot volume (goal/shot ratio separately identified).

For Forwards: 1 additional NPG/90 → ≈ 35–50% wage premium (on log scale)
