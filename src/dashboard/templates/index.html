Three Results Worth Knowing

What the thesis found

0.88
PSR Persistence — Ligue 1
PSR enforcement froze wages in Ligue 1. Mispricing persistence jumped from 0.33 to 0.88 — contracts locked into inefficiency regardless of performance. The most significant result: t=17.3, p<1×10⁻⁶⁴.
→ See full hypothesis test
63–71%
Wage Variance Explained
Position-specific OLS models explain 63–71% of log-wage variance using only publicly available stats. Age has a concave effect: wages peak around 29, then fall — consistent with declining productivity.
→ Explore the model
~20%
Players Overpaid Each Season
Roughly 1 in 5 players receives wages above what the model predicts. The gap is not random — it reflects brand value, negotiation power, and information asymmetry between clubs and agents.
→ See player rankings
